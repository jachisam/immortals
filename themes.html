  • Henry Cavill didn’t do any weight training when preparing for this film. His lean, highly defined physique was achieved through a tabata-style training regiment, consisting of only body weight exercises.

  • Scenes depicting the gods moving much quicker than mortals were shot at one thousand frames per second to achieve this effect.

  • According to Henry Cavill, all the male actors wanted to look their best when shirtless, which led to some healthy competition in the form of "best abs contests" to determine who had the best abs. Luke Evans said that Henry Cavill was hands-down the champion.

  • Luke Evans (Zeus) appeared a year earlier as the god Apollo in Clash of the Titans (2010).

  • The bronze bull torture device Hyperion uses to cook people to death in the film is an actual torture and execution device in ancient Greece called the “Brazen Bull.” According to Diodorus Sculls, it was invented by Perillos of Athens as a new means of executing criminals.

  • The filmmakers chose to change several aspects of the ancient Greek myths to reflect the fact that the Greeks themselves often revised their mythology to conform to new ideas and knowledge. For example, as noted in the special features, Zeus and Poseidon appear as young (rather than old) men.
