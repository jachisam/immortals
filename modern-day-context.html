Modern Day Context

Immortals as a medium for social commentary on political issues across the Globe

Film is a powerful and crucial medium to convey these key ideas and themes to people. With films, the directors create this experience where the audience goes on a journey and can relate to characters’ dilemmas, bringing the story to life into this modern contemporary space. The Greeks from oral tradition, plays and epics have had these overarching ideas about tyranny, the insatiable lust or greed, and how it can ruin society. Immortals gives a hopeful message that those tyrants can be defeated, but also serves as a vanguard to help prevent from future tyrants from taking power. Immortals parallels real-life events particularly with the Tunisian Revolution. In Tunisia, the revolution was galvanized with tragic death of Mohammed Bouazizi, a street vendor who set himself on fire in despair when his produce was stolen from him by corrupt local authorities. The Tunisian govern responded with repression of Ben Ali’s regime, one of the most brutal in the Arab world (Noor 1). Under the tyrannical regime, the Tunisian people lost their rights to free speech, media, and civil society organizations. The Tunisian people, the underdogs, were able to collectively defeat the oppressive and tyrannical regime.

Other Possible Modern Day Context:
To portray this event in the film, the oracle sisters being discovered scene paralleled the actual images of Mohamed Bouazizi’s remains. As the picture shown in the behind the scenes, one can see that Mohamed is charred and lifeless. In the film, Theseus and the Oracle run back to discover the King Hyperion’s iron bull over the raging fire. Discovering that the oracle sisters are placed in the bull, Theseus knocks over the iron bull and discovers the sisters. Just like Mohamed, the girls are lifeless and stuck, while their skin is charred from being roasted alive. Mohamed’s death galvanized the people to take action against the Ben Ali’s oppressive Tunisian regime, which is paralleled with the sisters being discovered right before the major climatic battle of Theseus’s men against Hyperion’s tyrannical army. The other major depiction is the comparison of Theseus and his men and of the people of Tunisia rebelling against the government. In the film, the soldiers, right before they are to face Hyperion’s men, are fearful, but they realize that this is battle that must be fought in order to protect their homes and families. They raise their swords and shields with ear-deafening roars just as we see in the images of the Tunisian people holding their flag thunderously chanting and fighting for their own freedom.

Directoral Choices

In the film, the underdog theme is very apparent through Theseus. Theseus, in the film, is a “bastard” and grows up in lowest class of society, shown in the scenes where the soldiers were taking the upper classes to safety from Hyperion’s men. Yet, this underdog was the one chosen by Zeus and is the main protagonist of the film fighting against tyranny for their freedom. He overcomes major hurdles such as the loss of his mother, working the mines, and fighting against a seemingly never-ending army. Theseus serves as the symbol of an underdog and how the underdog can win against tyranny, which is even more emphasized with Henry Cavil playing Superman in the next film. Superman highlights this because he plays the “outsider” that always fights for the underdog. Throughout the journey, the audience is able to see that this underdog, from the lowest of the bottom class, is able to defeat this monstrous tyrant, which plays into the social commentary aspect of the film. The underdog motif in the film gives hope that tyranny can be defeated and warns others the dangers of tyranny.
